Gwillim Grind

I hope you like hill climbs....

Between July and September, when the hiking trail is clear?

Day 1

Start at the Dam, possibly with a pint, and ride a mere 40km with around 200m of elevation gain. Northwards up the rail trail, past Passmore and in towards the Valhallas.

Night 1

After a stupidly easy day, chill at the Little Slocan River rec site for the night.

Day 2

A leisurely 20km biking distance and a paltry 940m elevation gain to the Gwillim Lakes hike trailhead. Stash the bikes and begin the ramble up to the lakes camp site. It’s only a 5.5km hike with another 500m of elevation gain. 1440m of up in a day is fine, eh?! You get a night’s rest at the top after all…

Night 2

After soaking your toasted legs in the lake, sleep like a baby in the heart of the Valhallas, at the Gwillim Lakes camp site.

Day 3

So much down….. Hike back down to the bikes and then a brake burner, all the way to Slocan, for a beer. With a casual cool down on the rail trail back to the Dam…. for a beer.

Options to make it easier

  1. Start the route closer to the high point… like park at Passmore or something?
  2. On day 1, pedal further up the road towards the Gwillim Lakes trailhead to reduce the climb the following day… I’m sure there’s somewhere alright to sleep up there somewhere.
  3. On day 2, instead of riding to Slocan and back down, just head back the way we rode in.
  4. Add another camp night at Slocan, to take it super chill and break up the long distance… even though the bulk of it is downhill.
  5. Stay at home and watch netflix.

Options to make it HARDER!

  1. Start from Nelson… bike down Granite road and hit the rail trail from there.
  2. After coming down from Gwillim Lakes, head North-West towards Farquier, get the ferry to Needles and find a way to ride South towards Castlegar, joining up with the Columbia and Western rail trail, on the West side of Arrow lake.
